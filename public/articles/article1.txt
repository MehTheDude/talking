The Sun: An Astronomical Oversight

I have a bone to pick with whoever designed this solar system. The sun? Way too big. Like, excessively, unnecessarily, overwhelmingly massive.

Let's look at the numbers, because I'm a reasonable person who deals in facts. The sun makes up 99.86% of all the mass in our entire solar system. That's not balance. That's hoarding.

You're telling me that Jupiter, Saturn, all the planets, every asteroid, every moon, every speck of cosmic dust COMBINED barely scratches 0.14%? Meanwhile the sun is just sitting there, taking up all the space, demanding attention, burning at millions of degrees like it's the main character.

And the arrogance. The sheer arrogance of a celestial body that can fit 1.3 million Earths inside it. What do you even need that much volume for? Hydrogen fusion? Okay, cool, but maybe leave some room for the rest of us.

The worst part? You can't even look at it. Try to have a polite conversation with the sun and you'll go blind. Rude.

I'm not saying we should downsize. I'm just saying a little proportionality would be nice. Maybe the sun could have shared some of that hydrogen with Jupiter. Made it a real star instead of a failed one. But no. The sun had to take it all.

And don't get me started on solar flares. "Oh look, I'm ejecting particles into space because I have too much energy." Control yourself.

Anyway, 3/10. Would not recommend this solar system layout.
