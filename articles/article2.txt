The Moon: A Disappointing Companion

I looked up last night and felt genuinely let down. The moon? Tiny. Embarrassingly small.

Here's what I was promised: a celestial companion, a nightlight for Earth's dark hours, something to inspire poets and werewolves alike. Here's what I got: a pathetic little rock that only LOOKS big because it's close. Don't be fooled.

You could fit 64 million moons inside the sun. SIXTY-FOUR MILLION. That's not a celestial body, that's a rounding error.

And what does it actually do? "Oh, it controls the tides." Barely. The ocean just shrugs and moves a few feet twice a day. My bathtub has more gravitational pull when I unplug the drain.

The moon is so small that when it tries to block the sun during an eclipse, it has to get the positioning EXACTLY right or it just looks like someone took a tiny bite out of a cookie. Get it together, moon.

We gave it the coolest name in the solar system and it did nothing with it. "Moon" sounds mysterious, powerful. But then you look closer and it's just gray dust and craters. No atmosphere. No life. Just vibes, and not even good ones.

I'm not asking for Jupiter-size. But maybe something with a little dignity? Something that doesn't get completely upstaged by a decent sunset?

The moon thinks it's our partner, but really it's just that friend who shows up uninvited, stands in the corner, and reflects everyone else's light.

0/10. Please return it and get a refund.
