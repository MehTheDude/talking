The Ocean: Please Drink Some Water

I went to the beach yesterday and accidentally swallowed a mouthful of ocean water. You know what I tasted? Regret. Disappointment. And approximately 35 grams of dissolved salt per liter.

The ocean is too salty. This is not up for debate.

Here's a thought experiment: If you left a glass of water on your nightstand for a million years, would it become undrinkable sludge? No. Because the ocean is doing something wrong.

And for what? Why so salty? The fish live there. Do they like it? No. They're just stuck. Evolution failed them. Imagine never being able to quench your thirst properly. Just swimming around in brine forever, getting that dried-out white film on your scales.

I did the math. The ocean contains about 50 quadrillion tons of dissolved salt. Spread that over all the land on Earth and you'd have a layer 500 feet thick. That's not "a little seasoning." That's a disaster.

Freshwater fish look at the ocean and say "absolutely not." And they're right. Even evolution noped out of that environment. "You want us to live WHERE? In concentrated salt water? No thanks, we'll stay in this pond where it's reasonable."

And don't tell me "it's for the currents" or "it affects the climate." I don't care. Desalination plants exist. We have the technology. We could fix this.

Instead we just accept it. Generation after generation, just accepting that 71% of our planet is covered in undrinkable, eye-stinging, wound-burning salt water.

The ocean needs to drink some water and calm down. 2/10. Would not swim.
